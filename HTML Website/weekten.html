Task 1

Evaluating Websites Against Accessibility Standards

Source - "Digital Divide"

"Wave Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool"

  • A link contains no text
  • Very low contrast between text and background colours
  • An embedded or linked YouTube video is present
  • Alternative text is null or empty (alt="") on a spacer image
  • A heading level is skipped
  • An aria-label or aria-labelledby attribute is present

"WebAIM Contrast Checker"

Contrast Ration - 2.51:1

"WebAIM Text, Background and Link Checker"

Link to body text - 2.95:1 (FAIL)

Link to background - 7.1:1 (PASS)

Body text to background - 21:1 (PASS)

"Readability Test Tool"

  • Average reading ease of 29.8 of 100
  • Age range is 20 to 21 year olds
  • 125 complex words
  • 21.74% of complex words

Task 2

Record of Findings and Reflection

Critical Assessment of Multimedia Website

From my research, this website targets young adults who are between the age group of 20 to 21 years old. The website contains various multimedia elements like images, videos, and website links that are positive additions to the website. It offers a complex take on the information presented. However, some of the HTML aspects of the website are incorrect, like skipped heading levels and empty/null alternative text for multimedia elements. The website’s accessibility is quite poor as there is a very low contrast between text and background colours making it difficult for users to read.

Reflective Statement

Ensuring accessibility is crucial in web design as it enables a website to be inclusive for all users. For my multimedia website, I have chosen a calming pastel colour scheme for the background of each HTML page. The body text is in a darker shade of the same colour, while the titles and links are in basic black text. I have opted for Times New Roman font as it is highly accessible and widely-used. I tested my colour scheme on the “WebAIM: Link Contrast Checker” to ensure that each colour passed the accessibility standards.

Task 3

Forms of Accessibility for my Multimedia Website

All images that are used on my multimedia website will have alt tags that accurately describe the subject and context of the image. This is especially helpful for visually impaired users who rely on screen readers to navigate the web.

Headings and subheadings will be used to create a clear content structure that makes it easier for users to follow along. This not only benefits users but also helps search engines navigate the content of the page.

Adding captions to videos is crucial for ensuring dead or hard-of-hearing users can access and understand the content. Its a simple but effective way to make my website more inclusive.
